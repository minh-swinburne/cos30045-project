1. Global Patterns of Alcohol Consumption by Type

This map offers a visual comparison of alcohol consumption rates across countries by type (wine, beer, spirits). It highlights cultural preferences and consumption patterns globally.

Sources:

  • 2023 OECD Health Statistics, Alcohol Consumption: Total alcohol consumption in pure litres of alcohol per capita (15+) in OECD countries, 1960-2023
  • WHO, Alcohol levels of consumption: RECORDED alcohol consumption in pure litres of alcohol per capita (15+), 1961-2020, by beverage type
  • WHO, recorded and unrecorded alcohol consumption in pure litres of alcohol per capita (15+)

Notes:

  • Recorded alcohol consumption refers to the consumption of alcohol that is recorded in official statistics, while unrecorded alcohol consumption refers to the consumption of alcohol that is not officially recorded.
  • The data is presented in pure litres of alcohol per capita (15+), which provides a standardized measure of alcohol consumption across countries.
  • The consumption of alcohol is broken down by type (wine, beer, spirits) to show the relative popularity of each type of alcohol in different countries.

3. Correlation Between Alcohol Consumption and Health Outcomes

This bubble chart shows the relationship between alcohol consumption and various health and social consequences, enabling a nuanced view of its impacts by country and gender.

Sources:

  • WHO, Alcohol Harms and Consequences - Mortality: Alcohol-attributable fractions, prevalence of death (%) in 2019, by cause and sex
  • IHME GBD 2021, Alcohol-related Deaths (+15) in 2019, by cause and sex.

Notes:

  • The bubble chart visualizes the relationship between alcohol consumption and health outcomes, such as mortality, by country, cause, and sex.
  • The size of the bubble represents the number of deaths or the prevalence of death (%) attributable to alcohol consumption for a specific cause, while the color indicates the level of alcohol consumption.
  • The chart allows for comparisons between different causes of death and providing insights into the health and social consequences of alcohol consumption.

5. Alcohol-Related Policies Across Countries

This geo chart provides a comparative view of alcohol-related policies, including taxes, prices, and restrictions, across countries.

Sources:

  • WHO, Road safety : blood alcohol concentration (BAC) limit: Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC) limit for drivers in 2017 by driver type

Notes:

  • The geo chart visualizes the variation in alcohol-related policies across countries, including blood alcohol concentration (BAC) limits for drivers, excise taxes on alcoholic beverages, and restrictions on alcohol use in public places.
  • The color intensity on the map represents the level of policy stringency, with darker shades indicating stricter policies and lighter shades indicating more lenient policies.
  • The chart provides insights into the regulatory environment for alcohol consumption and the effectiveness of policies in reducing alcohol-related harms.
